• Reflections Gallery


When I mirror some of my images in Photoshop, I observe all sorts of crazy psychedelic stuff including faces and hidden features. This is especially so in good light and focus, and high contrast (sun, laser, LED, etc.). Also rough fractal-like natural forms work the best. Sources include water, clouds, trees, fire, crystal, and ice. I love the way the light reflects on the images and how the patterns emerge in the symmetry of reflection and rotation. I often see geometrical patterns, symmetry, and even shapes like faces in the photographs that I have reflected and blended in Photoshop.

Pareidolia is the term for seeing faces in things. This is a tendency to impose a meaningful interpretation on a nebulous visual stimulus such as a face in the clouds or within a crystal. Scientists have discovered that observing patterns and faces is naturally part of what our brains do to create meaning and context. Over the years I have discovered that the photography of the natural elements at different scales of reality can reveal hidden information. These give insights into the many streams of visionary and spiritual artwork that are very popular today.
